Title: Director, Performance Analytics
CS Title: Computer Systems Manager
Agency: NYC Department of Buildings
Salary: $60,435 - $120,000 (Annual)

Job Description

The NYC Department of BBuildings is seeking a Director of Performance Analytics. This position will lead all aspects of the performance measurement program at the Department, building on existing efforts, assessing progress to date, and developing and implementing a plan for the future. It will be critical for the successful candidate to envision an effective partnership with Spatial Analysis, Predictive Risk and Research and Analysis efforts.


Duties will include:
  • Developing and modifying Key Performance Indicators to ensure the agency is meeting critical goals.
  • Briefing the Commissioner and senior staff in performance measurement meetings including developing relevant presentations, metrics, and strategies for effectively communicating with operational leaders to ensure productive conversations about performance.
  • Researching and implementing best practices in performance measurement to advance the Deparment's efforts to improve performance.
  • Analyzing complex data sets with business considerations in mind and presenting findings in an easy to consume manner.
  • Using technical applications for database administration, planning of data and information processing, and user services./li>
  • Conducting quality assurance on analysis of the Performance Analytics unit and willingness to conduct quality assurance on peer analysis.
  • Leading and managing a team of data analysts.
  • Performing professional managerial work in the planning, organizing, and controlling of major technical initiatives.
  • Partnering in a data warehouse modernization effort .
  • Serving as a technical resource for special projects and technology initiatives.
  • Special projects as assigned by the Chief Analytics Officer

  2. A baccalaureate degree from an accredited college and four years of experience as described in "1" above; or
  3. A four-year high school diploma or its educational equivalent approved by a State's department of education or recognized accrediting organization and six years of experience as described in "1" above; or
  4. A satisfactory combination of education and experience equivalent to "1", "2" or "3" above. However, all candidates must have at least a four-year high school diploma or its educational equivalent approved by a State's department of education or recognized accrediting organization and must possess at least three years of experience as described in "1" above, including the 18 months of administrative, managerial, executive or supervisory experience as described in "1" above
